## ARCHITECTURAL STORY

### The Form Concept
- Geometry generated by **subtracting a spiraling quadrant** from a simple prismatic volume
- The subtracted portion is **incrementally rotated** at each higher level
- Result: two **hyperbolic paraboloid reinforced-concrete walls** (the flared/torque walls)
- These walls extend the **full 412 m height** and act as both structure and facade

### The Dual Facade Logic
- **North/East/West faces:** Extensive insulating glass — transparency toward the Gulf
- **South face:** Monolithic Jura limestone — opacity toward the desert
- **Deep angled windows** on south wall: frames city/peninsula views while blocking direct sun
- **Flared wall ribbons** spiral continuously from ground to apex — continuing infinitely upward

### The Engineering Achievement
- One of the **few reinforced concrete super high-rises** (most supertalls use steel)
- **Gravity-induced torsion** from the asymmetric flared walls required novel structural solutions
- **Rotational correction program** during construction — formwork twisted slightly per floor
- **Creep and shrinkage** of concrete studied over 10-year post-completion period
- **Wind design controlled** (low seismicity region) — BMT Fluid Mechanics as wind engineer
- **Lamella buckling analysis** — 4-model progressive study increased buckling capacity by 2×
- **289 piles in 7 phases** — construction proceeded while design was still being finalized

### The Site
- **Location:** Sharq District, Kuwait City — north of retail podium, east of major road
- **Gulf views:** Primary amenity — offices face north, west, east toward Arabian Gulf
- **Site constraint:** Podium and parking were already under construction when SOM was engaged
- **Fast-track:** SOM commenced design while foundation excavation was already in progress
- **Design code:** International Building Code 2003 (IBC 2003) + ACI 318-02M
